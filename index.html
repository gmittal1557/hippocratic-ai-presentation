The Problem

1 in 5 colonoscopies has inadequate prep.

  • — Patients still pay the cost. A day off work, a gallon of laxative, and a repeat visit.

What it does

  • Coaches prep, confirms meds, triages symptoms
  • Confirms logistics: nothing by mouth, escort, arrival
  • Writes back to Epic: note, Flag, Observation, In-Basket

What it deliberately does not do

  • Make clinical decisions or interpret labs
  • Address transport or financial barriers (Phase 2 agents)
  • Replace the GI nurse; escalates to one when criteria are met

Four design choices.

01

Pre-load, then verify.

All FHIR reads happen at 3 PM. The agent verbally re-confirms critical items — meds, escort — during the call. Chart drives the questions; patient answers drive the flags.

02

Integration delivers, agent decides.

The integration pulls every resource on the chart. Which conditions, meds, or labs are clinically relevant is decided by the customer's filters at the agent layer — not at fetch.

03

Customer-authorized service.

IT approves it once. Read access to nine FHIR resources, write to four. Scoped to the GI service line.

04

When the pre-load fails.

If a patient's chart can't be loaded at 3 PM: skip that call, alert the nurse pool, trigger the customer's existing SMS reminder.

Deployment

Open deployment questions.

What I'd resolve with the customer before going live.

01 Week 1

What's each physician's prep protocol?

Mapped with the GI ops lead. Per-physician profile.

02 Week 1

Who takes the escalation?

After-hours nurse pool, mapped with the informaticist. Tested end-to-end before go-live.

03 Week 1

Where does the note land?

Default: Telephone Encounter linked to tomorrow's procedure, routed to ordering physician + nurse pool. Confirmed with the IT lead per customer.

Roadmap

Success and next steps.

What we measure, and what we build next.

How we'd know it's working.

Did it work?

  • Did patients arrive with clean preps? Bowel-prep quality scored at the procedure.
  • Did fewer procedures get cancelled day-of? Same-day cancellation rate vs. baseline.

Is the system running?

  • Are we reaching patients? Connection rate on outbound calls.
  • Are they staying on the line? Completion rate among connected calls.

Is it safe?

  • Are nurses correcting the agent's notes? Override rate as a proxy for accuracy.
  • Any patient safety incidents? Adverse-event count. Target: zero.

What we'd build next.

Coverage

A logistics agent and an anxiety agent.

Same patient. Different failure modes. Covers 70% of misses, not 30%.

Market

A lighter version for community GI practices.

Same agent. Lighter deployment. Smaller volumes, simpler EHRs.

Journey

Day-of and Day-after calls.

Arrival confirmation. Recovery check-in. Same patient, same pipeline.
